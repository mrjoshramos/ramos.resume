4. Timeline

The Early Years

    The headline "Two Decades of Work" is a nod at the fact that my sales career started extremely early. The first sales I can recall happened when I was 8 years old selling Pokemon cards door to door to other kids in the neighborhood. This was not a very profitable endeavor, but it was wildly successful because it funded my Pokemon collecting hobby.

    This narrative would continue throughout my teenage years. Everything from selling car washes and mowing lawns, to selling kitchen knives with Cutco during the summer. Sales and entrepreneurship is wired into my DNA. This has carried over into the last decade of work as I have taken those entrepreurial tendencies to execute in every role I have held.

The Last Decade

    Telecom

    The first 2 years of my tech sales career were in telecom - B2B wireless sales and then networking. I learned a lot of great things in this period, but I didn't feel challenged in my day to day.

    Colocation & IaaS

    Through relationships I had built, I found my way into a company called Peer 1 Hosting (later known as Cogeco Peer 1). This was my first introduction to Hosted Infrastructure aka Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS). I would spend several years selling colocation and Vmware based IaaS solutions to a variety of customers ranging from SMB into Mid-Market / Commerical businesses. Some deals were $2K/mo. while others were $50k/mo. or more.

    Hyperscaler - Microsoft

    The last three years has been spent at Microsoft, where I moved from SMC with 90 accounts into Strategics with only one Enterprise account and taking said account from $26M/yr. to over $50M/yr. This time at Microsoft has been a master-class on closing large deals through incredible competition, while learning how to navigate a $2T organization. I'm 10x better today than this time 3 years ago, and I still have much to learn.
